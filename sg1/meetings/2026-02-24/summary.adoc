=== 2. Final Review of Definitions

Ge Peng presented the results of an action from the previous meeting: a review of each term proposed for removal, checking whether it appears in the Manual provisions and in the Stewardship Maturity Matrix (WMO-No. 1328).

Two terms remained for team decision:

==== Data Portability

Data portability does not appear anywhere in the Manual provisions, only in the maturity matrix. William noted these terms were originally included because they were used in the maturity matrix, but the focus has shifted. The team agreed to remove data portability from the definitions and place it in the glossary.

**Decision**: Remove from definitions; retain in glossary.

==== Data Analysis

Data analysis appears only once in the provisions, at section 3.7.1, in a generic context ("good practice in climate data management and analysis"). The current definition is broad: _the process of inspecting, cleansing, transforming and modeling data with the goal of generating useful information…_

Peter Thorne noted the term is so broad as to be largely meaningless as a standalone definition — it could encompass quality control, homogenisation, gridding, interpolation, indices, and more. He supported moving it to the glossary only, not the definitions section.

Etna Cervantes argued for retaining it, noting that many hydrological variables (e.g. river flow, evapotranspiration) are derived/transformed quantities that fall under data analysis, and the definition helps members understand what they need to fulfil requirements.

William supported keeping it in the glossary for now, noting that in a future broader edition the definition may be refined as "climate data analysis."

**Decision**: Keep in glossary (not in the definitions section); may be revisited when the scope of 1238 is broadened.

==== Centennial Observing Stations and Long-Term Stations

William raised the Centennial Observing Station definition and asked whether it could be broadened to reference other categories of long-term observing stations (e.g., stations with 75 years of record, intended to encourage members to maintain stations until they reach centennial status).

David confirmed Chapter 5 of the Manual uses both "Centennial Observing Station" and "long-term observing station," providing a hook for the latter definition. The Guidelines document is referenced from that chapter. David also noted that a discussion with DWD colleagues the previous day raised the question of whether long-term and centennial stations should be required to be registered in OSCAR/Surface, as not all are currently registered.

William agreed that OSCAR/Surface registration should be a requirement. David indicated this could be explored as a new provision.

William asked that proposed text for the long-term station definition be submitted via email to the whole group.

==== Essential Climate Variables (ECV)

Liu Yiming noted that the ECV definition is important because the Manual concerns the management of climate data, and ECVs are a key category. He argued the Manual should make clear that ECVs deserve particular attention (e.g. for storage, quality control flags, reference metadata), and that the Manual should reference the authoritative definition rather than redefine it.

Peter Thorne informed the group that GCOS is revising its ECV definitions as part of an ECV rationalisation process. The GCOS Steering Committee needs to formally approve the revised definitions, which is expected within 6–12 months. Peter shared the revised definitions in the meeting chat:

* **Essential Climate Variable (ECV)**: a well-defined quantity that critically contributes to the characterisation of Earth's climate.
* **ECV Quantity**: a property or phenomenon whose magnitude can be expressed as a number; ECV quantities can be physical, chemical, or biological.

The team agreed that if these definitions are incorporated into the Manual on WIGOS, the Manual on 1238 should reference WIGOS rather than independently defining them.

David confirmed he would raise the ECV definition at the WIGOS meeting immediately following this call (with Kruno/Belen) to ensure alignment.

==== WMO-No. 49 and Cross-Manual Definitions

The discussion also touched on terms where the current version of the Manual says "see definition in WMO-No. 49." David clarified that these definitions already exist in WMO-No. 49. The team could recommend moving them fully into 1238 and deleting them from WMO-No. 49, if the terms are specific enough to this Manual rather than cross-cutting. William noted six to eight definitions fall into this category.

David also noted a request from Congress to move definitions from WMO-No. 49 into the relevant Manuals, which supports this approach.

=== 3. INFCOM Submission

David reported on the INFCOM process:

* The INFCOM Management Group meeting is in two weeks. The list of papers to be submitted to INFCOM will be discussed but not in detail.
* This work (the update to Manual 1238) will be included.
* Given the work involves harmonising and clarifying existing regulations rather than adding new provisions, it is expected to be submitted for **non-debate** approval — any member can request it be brought to the floor, but otherwise it will be submitted for approval without debate.
* This decision will be confirmed at the INFCOM Management Group session.
